# 🎓 Instructor Guide - Marketing Budget Simulation

## Overview

This simulation teaches students marketing budget allocation using the DDOM framework (Discover, Try, Buy, Use, Renew) based on real-world SaaS business principles.

---

## Learning Objectives

By completing this simulation, students will:

1. **Understand Customer Lifecycle Management**
   - Discover → Try → Buy → Use → Renew framework
   - How each stage impacts business metrics

2. **Practice Budget Allocation Under Constraints**
   - Trade-offs between competing priorities
   - Short-term vs. long-term investment decisions

3. **Experience Compound Returns**
   - How fixed investments build over time
   - The power of consistent strategy

4. **Learn Retention Economics**
   - Why retention is a growth multiplier
   - The cost of churn vs. cost of retention

5. **Develop Strategic Planning Skills**
   - Multi-quarter planning horizon
   - Adapting strategy based on results

---

## Class Structure Recommendations

### **Option 1: Individual Assignment (3-4 hours)**

**Week 1:**
- Introduce DDOM framework (30 min lecture)
- Demo simulation platform (15 min)
- Assign: Complete 8 quarters, submit report

**Week 2:**
- Students present their strategies (15 min each)
- Class discussion on different approaches
- Debrief on key learnings

### **Option 2: Team Competition (2 weeks)**

**Week 1:**
- Form teams of 3-4 students
- Each team runs simulation independently
- Mid-week checkpoint: Review Q1-Q2 results

**Week 2:**
- Complete remaining quarters
- Teams present final results
- Award prizes for:
  - Highest total ARR
  - Best strategic insight
  - Most creative approach

### **Option 3: Live Classroom Session (90 minutes)**

**Structure:**
1. Introduction (15 min)
2. Students run Q1-Q2 individually (20 min)
3. Small group discussion (15 min)
4. Complete Q3-Q4 2021 (20 min)
5. Class debrief on 2021 patterns (10 min)
6. Optional: Start 2022 (10 min)

## Common Student Mistakes & How to Guide

### **Mistake #1: Over-indexing on BUY Phase**

**What they do:** Put 60-70% of budget on BUY
**Result:** Diminishing returns, poor results
**How to guide:** 
- "What happens to the funnel if nobody discovers you?"
- Show them the balance in winning strategy (40% max on BUY)

### **Mistake #2: Ignoring Fixed Investments**

**What they do:** 100% variable spend all quarters
**Result:** ROI stays low, never breaks through
**How to guide:**
- "Why do you think Q2 always jumps?"
- Explain compound effect of fixed investments

### **Mistake #3: Cutting RENEW Too Low**

**What they do:** Put 0-2% on RENEW
**Result:** Retention tanks, Cancel ARR spikes
**How to guide:**
- "What's the cost of acquiring a new customer vs. retaining one?"
- Show retention improvement saves millions in churn

### **Mistake #4: Changing Strategy Every Quarter**

**What they do:** Completely different allocation each quarter
**Result:** No compounding, inconsistent results
**How to guide:**
- "How do investments compound if you keep changing?"
- Show the 2021 consistency pattern

### **Mistake #5: Panicking in Q1**

**What they do:** Abandon strategy after Q1 underperformance
**Result:** Never see Q2 breakthrough
**How to guide:**
- "Q1 is designed to underperform - why?"
- Explain foundation-building phase

1. Why did you get the result you got?
2. What surprised you?
3. What will you change for Q2?

### **Post-Q4 2021:**
4. How did your strategy evolve?
5. What was your best quarter? Why?
6. What pattern did you notice across quarters?

### **Post-Q4 2022:**
7. How did 2022 differ from 2021?
8. What was your biggest strategic insight?
9. If you could restart, what would you do differently?

### **Final Debrief:**
10. How does retention rate impact total ARR?
11. Why is balance across phases important?
12. What's the value of fixed investments?
13. How does this apply to real businesses?

## Teaching Tips

### **Before Class:**
- Run through simulation yourself to understand patterns
- Prepare 2-3 strategy examples to show
- Set up projection for live demo

### **During Introduction:**
- Use real company examples (Netflix, Spotify, Adobe)
- Explain why each phase matters
- Clarify variable vs. fixed distinction

### **During Simulation:**
- Circulate and ask guiding questions
- Avoid giving direct answers - let them discover
- Highlight interesting strategies to the class

### **During Debrief:**
- Start with student insights before sharing yours
- Use actual student data for discussion
- Connect to real-world business cases

## Troubleshooting Student Issues

### **"I can't hit the target!"**
- Normal! Very few hit 100%
- Focus on learnings, not just score
- 85%+ is excellent performance

### **"The simulation is broken!"**
- Likely over-indexing one phase
- Check their phase balance
- Review fixed investment timing

### **"Q1 is too hard!"**
- Designed that way (foundation building)
- Q2 will be better
- Trust the process

### **"My retention is too low!"**
- Increase RENEW budget
- Check USE phase (engagement helps retention)
- Fixed investments in RENEW compound
